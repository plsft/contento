1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and supply information to site owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies:

  • Persistent Cookies: Remain on your device after you close your browser. They are activated each time you visit the website that created them. Persistent cookies have an expiration date set in their code and are deleted when that date is reached, or when you manually delete them.
  • Session Cookies: Are temporary and are deleted from your device when you close your web browser. They are used to maintain your session while you navigate between pages on a website.

Cookies can be set by the website you are visiting ("first-party cookies") or by third parties, such as analytics or advertising services ("third-party cookies").

3. How to Control Cookies

You have several options for controlling and managing cookies:

Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit the Service, you will see a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. You can change your preferences at any time through the cookie settings accessible in the footer of our website.

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically configure your browser to:

  • Block all cookies or only third-party cookies.
  • Delete existing cookies when you close your browser.
  • Notify you when a website tries to set a cookie.
  • Accept or reject cookies on a case-by-case basis.

Please note that blocking or deleting essential cookies may prevent you from using certain features of the Service, such as logging in to your account.

Browser-Specific Instructions

For instructions on managing cookies in your specific browser, please visit:

  • Chrome: Settings > Privacy and Security > Cookies and other site data
  • Firefox: Settings > Privacy & Security > Cookies and Site Data
  • Safari: Preferences > Privacy > Manage Website Data
  • Edge: Settings > Cookies and site permissions > Manage and delete cookies and site data
